01

Understanding the Core Combat System

Welcome to the battlefield! Super Smash Bros. Ultimate revolutionizes traditional fighting games by replacing health bars with a unique damage percentage system. Instead of depleting your opponent's health, you accumulate damage shown as a percentage beneath each fighter's portrait. The core objective is to rack up damage on opponents and then launch them beyond the stage's blast zones. The higher your damage percentage climbs, the farther you'll fly when struck by attacks. At low percentages, you might shrug off powerful hits, but at 150% or above, even a modest strike can send you soaring into the stratosphere.

The game runs at 60 frames per second, meaning one frame equals approximately 16.7 milliseconds. This frame-based timing system governs everything from attack speed to defensive options. Understanding frame data helps you recognize which moves come out quickly for pressure and which attacks leave you vulnerable. To prevent players from spamming the same powerful attack repeatedly, the game employs stale move negation—an internal counter tracks your last ten moves, and repeated use of the same attack makes it progressively weaker in both damage and knockback.

// Key Terminology
Damage % → Your vulnerability meter (0-999%)
Knockback → Launch force applied when hit
KO/Stock → Losing a life by being launched off-stage
Frame Data → Technical timing properties (60 fps)
Stale Move Negation → Damage penalty for repetition

The Four Ways to Score a KO:

  1. Launch opponents beyond side blast zones (left/right)
  2. Send them above the top blast zone
  3. Spike or meteor smash them below the bottom blast zone
  4. (Stamina Mode only) Reduce their HP to zero
02

Attack Types & Movement Options

Every fighter in Ultimate possesses a diverse arsenal organized into distinct attack categories. Ground attacks include jabs (pressing A without directional input), tilts (A button with slight stick movement), and smash attacks (A button with strong stick flick or simultaneous press). Jabs are your fastest option, perfect for interrupting approaches or starting combos. Tilts include forward-tilt, up-tilt, and down-tilt, performed by gently tilting the control stick and pressing attack.

Aerial combat introduces five unique airborne attacks that define Ultimate's dynamic gameplay. These include Fair (forward-air), Bair (back-air), Uair (up-air), Dair (down-air), Nair (neutral-air), and for certain characters, Zair (grab button aerial). Mastering aerial drift allows you to weave between platforms, chase opponents off-stage, and execute devastating combos.

// Attack Input Notation
Jab = A button (neutral, no direction)
F-Tilt / U-Tilt / D-Tilt = Slight stick + A
F-Smash / U-Smash / D-Smash = Strong flick + A
Fair / Bair / Uair / Dair / Nair = Aerial attacks
Neutral-B / Side-B / Up-B / Down-B = Special moves

Movement Tech Checklist:

  • Dash dancing: Quickly alternating dash directions
  • Short hop: Minimal jump height for fast aerials
  • Fast fall: Hold down during airborne descent
  • Wavedashing: L-cancel technique for reduced lag
  • Ledge trumping: Stealing occupied ledge
03

Defensive Mechanics & Survival Techniques

Survival in Ultimate demands mastery of defensive options and understanding of knockback mechanics. Shields grant invincibility to shielded portions of your character but shrink with each hit sustained. You can angle your shield to cover vulnerable areas, but overusing shield leaves you susceptible to grabs. Upon respawning after a KO, you receive temporary invincibility—use these seconds strategically.

Directional Influence (DI) is your most crucial survival tool—by holding the control stick perpendicular to your launch trajectory, you can shift your knockback angle to survive longer. When launched horizontally, hold up to angle your trajectory higher. Smash DI involves rapidly flicking the control stick during multi-hit attacks to escape combos.

// Defensive Options
Shield → Invincibility bubble (shrinks when hit)
Spot Dodge → Brief ground intangibility
Roll → Moving intangibility frames
Air Dodge → Airborne evasion (limited uses)
Parry → Perfect shield timing

DI Survival Guide:

  1. Horizontal launch → DI upward or perpendicular
  2. Vertical launch → DI toward stage center
  3. Diagonal launch → DI away from angle
  4. Multi-hit moves → Smash DI by stick flicking
  5. Never DI toward blast zones
04

Edge-Guarding & Off-Stage Combat

The edge game separates casual players from competitive masters. Edge-guarding refers to preventing opponents from returning to stage after being launched off-stage. Gimping involves intercepting recovery attempts early, KO'ing opponents at low damage percentages. Meteor smashes send opponents straight downward, though victims can jump out with precise timing.

Ledge mechanics evolved significantly in Ultimate with ledge trumping, which allows you to steal an occupied edge. After grabbing ledge, you receive brief intangibility frames—use this invincibility to time your return safely. Advanced players intentionally release ledge, execute aerials, then re-grab to continue edge-guarding pressure.

// Edge-Guard Terminology
Gimping → Early KO during recovery
Meteor Smash → Downward spike (escapable)
Spike → True downward attack
Stage Spike → Bouncing off surfaces
Ledge Trump → Stealing occupied ledge

Edge-Guard Strategies:

  1. Position between opponent and ledge
  2. Use projectiles to disrupt recovery
  3. Drop off stage for aggressive intercepts
  4. Two-frame punish at ledge-grab moment
  5. Cover multiple recovery angles
05

Advanced Techniques & Competitive Concepts

Priority determines which attack wins when two moves collide—the system is damage-dependent, with moves needing 8% or more damage difference to beat each other. Understanding priority helps you challenge opponents' approaches. Grabs beat shields universally but lose to attacks, creating a rock-paper-scissors dynamic.

Jab locks occur when opponents lie grounded and you hit them with weak attacks, forcing them into a standard getup animation. Combos are sequences of attacks that opponents cannot escape from, requiring precise timing and understanding of hitstun. Mastering character-specific combos transforms random hits into consistent KO confirms.

// Priority System
Normal Priority → Damage diff less than 8% = clank
High Priority → Damage diff 8% or more = stronger wins
Grab Priority → Beats shields, loses to attacks
Aerial Priority → Speed-dependent collisions
Transcendent → Ignores other hitboxes (rare)

Advanced Tech Checklist:

  • Jab lock setups for guaranteed follow-ups
  • Pivot canceling for instant direction reversals
  • B-reverse and wavebounce for aerial momentum shifts
  • Perfect pivots for precise spacing control
  • Buffering inputs during animation lag
  • Tech chasing opponents' ground bounces
  • Understanding weight classes and knockback scaling
  • Rage mechanic: Higher damage increases knockback

Character Archetype Guide:

Zoners: Control space with projectiles (Samus, Snake)
Rush-down: Aggressive close-range pressure (Fox, Roy)
Grapplers: Command grab specialists (Bowser, Incineroar)
Bait-and-Punish: Defensive counterattackers (Lucina, Palutena)
All-Rounders: Balanced toolkits (Mario, Wolf)
